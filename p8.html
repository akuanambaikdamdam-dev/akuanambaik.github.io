Synopsis

A medical memoir capturing Dr. Stern's intense four-year residency training in psychiatry at Harvard Medical School. The narrative delves into the realities of inpatient psychiatric wards, the emotional tax of treating severe mental illnesses, and the personal maturation of doctors learning to treat the human mind.

Author Background

Dr. Adam Stern is an American psychiatrist and an Assistant Professor of Psychiatry at Harvard Medical School. He has written extensively for medical journals as well as mainstream publications like The New York Times and The Boston Globe. He wrote this book to give the public a transparent, empathetic look into the grueling process of psychiatric training and the human side of medical professionals.

Why Read This Book

It offers a rare, humanizing look behind the doors of psychiatric medicine. It serves as an engaging, dramatic, yet empathetic read on mental health care, making it an excellent pick for anyone interested in the medical field or human psychology.
