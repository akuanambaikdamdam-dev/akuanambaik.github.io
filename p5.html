Author: Baek Sehee
Publication Year:
2018 (South Korea)
Genre: Memoir /
Mental Health

Synopsis

A raw recording of a 12-week dialogue between the author and her psychiatrist while treating persistent, low-grade depression (dysthymia). The book features honest reflections on self-worth, social masking, relationships, and the paradox of feeling deeply empty while simultaneously desiring the comfort of everyday things—like eating street food.

Author Background

Baek Sehee graduated with a degree in creative writing before working for several years at a publishing house. She suffered from chronic dysthymia (persistent depressive disorder) and anxiety for over a decade. Frustrated by the lack of books that mirrored her specific mental state, she decided to record her actual psychiatric sessions and self-publish them via a crowdfunding campaign, which became a massive literary sensation in South Korea.

Why Read This Book

It offers deep comfort to anyone struggling with invisible mental health battles or mild anxiety. Its non-preachy, dialogue-driven format provides comfort, validating complex emotions and reassuring readers that it is okay to feel imperfect.
