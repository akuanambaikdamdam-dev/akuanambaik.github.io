Synopsis

The authors study the Japanese lifestyle concept of "Ikigai" (meaning "a reason for being" or "the reason to wake up in the morning"). Through interviews with centenarians in Okinawa—a global longevity hotspot—they explore the intersections of diet, mild physical activity, a sense of community, and finding personal purpose.

Author Background

Héctor García is a Spanish-born software engineer and writer who moved to Japan in 2004. He created a widely read blog about Japanese culture and technology before transitioning to writing full-time.

Francesc Miralles is an award-winning Spanish author, journalist, and musician who specializes in psychology and self-help. He has written numerous books spanning self-help, translation, and fiction.

Why Read This Book

It serves as a calm, life-affirming blueprint for longevity and mental peace. It encourages readers to slow down, discover what they love, what they are good at, and what the world needs, blending career fulfillment with daily happiness.
